by Abdulwasiu Bamidele Popoola¶

Investigation Overview¶

The goal of this work is to find out how some features affect the LoanStatus of borrowers in the Prosper Loan dataset. We also explored the relationship between some of the other features.

Some of the key inisghts gotten from this exploratory analysis include:

A borrowers LoanStatus is affected by the BorrowerAPR, BorrowerRate, Term etc.

There is a strong positve correlation between the BorrowerAPR and BorrowerRate.

The borrowers with Current loan status had longer EmploymentStatusDuration. Those who defaulted or past due dates had relatively smaller EmploymentStatusDuration.

Borrowers with Current or Completed loan status have relatively lower BorrowerRate while those who defaulted or were past due dates had higher BorrowerRate.

The BorrowerRate was highest amongst those who have Defaulted.

Most loans were for a Term of 36 months.

Dataset Overview¶

This data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 features, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others.

This data dictionary explains the variables in the data set.

The following features may help support our investigation into the feature of interest (i.e. LoanStatus): I expect that most of the other features will have an effect on the feature of interest. Some of these features include: Term, BorrowerAPR, BorrowerRate, Occupation, EmploymentStatus, EmploymentStatusDuration, IsBorrowerHomeowner.

I had to select the main features by subsetting the main dataset for the above features I used for this exploratory work. In addition, had to set the order of charting for the nominal categorical variables but not for the ordinal variables. For some features, I also had to set the bin sizes (in some cases by using numpy arange function) in order to get a better view of the distribution of the variables. I also had to select some features from the entire dataset to narrow down my exploration on them.

The datatypes of some features was also changed to the appropriate datatype to enable proper exploration and visualization with the appropriate charts.

For some features with too many values, I focused on visualizing the top and bottom part of the observations.

Most variables in the original DataFrame, loaded as df are of the numeric datatype. The dataset has 11393 observations and 81 features. The dataset has 50 variables of float64 datatype, 17 object, 11 int64 and 3 bool.

Our newly created subset DataFrame, named loans is our working dataset which has 23 numeric, 3 datatime64[ns], 1 object, 2 bool, and 9 category datatypes.

Our feature of interest, LoanStatus is a categorical variable with 12 main categories of borrowers. However the four highest are: Current making up about 49%of total borrowers, Completed constituting about 33%, Chargedoff makine up 10% and Defaulted making up about 4% of total borrowers by LoanStatus.

Visualization 1: Distribution of our feature of interest- LoanStatus¶

The chart shows that majority have a Loan Status of Current (about 49%) followed by Completed (about 33%), Chargedoff (about 10%), Defaulted (about 3%) and Past Due (1-15 days) (about 0.7%) and then the others.

Visualization 1: Relationship between LoanStatus and BorrowerAPR¶

Those with Current Loan Status have one of the relatively lowest mean BorrowerAPR. Those who defaulted or were past their paymemt due dates had higher BorrowerAPR. Thus the BorrowerAPR can be seen to affect the LoanStatus of borrowers.

Visualization 2: Relationship between LoanStatus and BorrowerRate¶

Borrowers with Current or Completed status have relatively lower BorrowerRate(interest rate) while those who defaulted or were past due dates had higher BorrowerRate.

Visualization 3: Relationship between LoanStatus and Term¶

Amongst all categories of LoanStatus, the commonest loan Term is 36 months, and particularly, amongst those borrowers with Completed and Current loan statuses. Amongst Current loan status borrowers, 60 months Term is the 2nd commnest.

Visualization 4: Relationship between LoanStatus and AvailableBankcardCredit¶

Those borrowers with Completed or Current LoanStatus have higher AvailableBankcardCredit. Those borrowers with status of Past Due (>120 days) had the least AvailableBankcardCredit.

Visualization 5: Relationship between BorrowerAPR and BorrowerRate¶

There is a positve correlation between the BorrowerAPR and BorrowerRate.

Visualization 6: Relationship between LoanStatus and EmploymentStatusDuration¶

This distribution has a lot of observed outliers. The borrowers with Current loan status had longer EmploymentStatusDuration. Those who Defaulted or past due dates had relatively smaller EmploymentStatusDuration.

Visualization 7: Relationship between LoanStatus and IsBorrowerHomeowner¶

Those borrowers without homes had a higher BorrowerRate.

Visualization 8: Relationship between LoanStatus and Term¶

The 12 months loan term had the lowest median BorrowerRate while both the 36 and 60 months terms had about thesame median BorrowerRate.

The 36 months Term had the highest BorrowerRate with some even shown as outliers. It also had the lowest rate amongst the three terms.

Visualization 9: Relationship between Term, IsBorrowerhomeowner and BorrowerRate¶

Borrowers without homes have a higher BorrowerRate for thesame loan Term.

Visualization 10: Relationship between LoanStatus, Term and BorrowerAPR¶

Those with Term of 12 months and LoanStatus of Defaulted had the highest BorrowerAPR. Amongst Current borrowers, there is no much difference in BorrowerAPR for all the three Terms

Visualization 11: Relationship between LoanStatus, Term and BorrowerRate¶

Those with Defaulted status had the highest BorrowerRate for those with Term of 12 months.

For Term of 12 months, those who had the lowest BorrowerRate were those with LoanStatus of FinalPaymentInProgress.

There is a progressive increase in LoanRate for those with statuses of Chargedoff, Completed, and Current with increasing loan Term. Amongst the Defaulted group, there doesn't appear to be much difference in BorrowerRate across the three loan Terms.

BorrowerRates appear to be higher for LoanStatuses of Defaulted onwards across all Terms.
